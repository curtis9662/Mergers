# Enterprise Security M&A: Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Duties

In an M&A context, the Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) workstream evaluates the target entity's foundational security controls, regulatory obligations, and risk management practices. This ensures the acquiring organization understands the legal, financial, and operational risks associated with the target’s security posture before, during, and after the transaction.

Below are the top 20 duties for an Enterprise Security M&A GRC service offering, categorized by the phases of the M&A lifecycle.

## Phase 1: Pre-Diligence & Strategic Alignment
1. **Define M&A GRC Scope:** Establish the regulatory and compliance baseline required for the transaction based on the target’s industry, geographic footprint, and the strategic goals of the acquisition.
2. **Determine Framework Applicability:** Identify the prevailing security frameworks (e.g., NIST CSF, ISO 27001, CIS Controls) used by both the parent and target entities to establish a baseline for gap analysis.
3. **Establish Secure Data Handling Protocols:** Implement and enforce Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and clean-room/Virtual Data Room (VDR) procedures to protect sensitive compliance artifacts during the diligence phase.

## Phase 2: Policy, Process, and Governance Review
4. **Policy Maturity Assessment:** Review the target entity’s core Information Security Policies, Acceptable Use Policies, and Data Handling Standards to evaluate their maturity and enforcement.
5. **Security Leadership & Board Reporting Evaluation:** Assess the reporting structure of the target's security function (e.g., CISO reporting lines) and review historical board of directors' security updates and meeting minutes.
6. **Exception Management Review:** Analyze the target's policy exception log to identify accepted systemic risks, long-term un-remediated vulnerabilities, or technical debt masquerading as accepted risk.
7. **Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery (BCP/DR) Governance:** Evaluate the existence, maintenance, and testing frequency of BCP and DR plans to ensure organizational resilience.

## Phase 3: Compliance and Data Privacy Evaluation
8. **Regulatory Adherence Assessment:** Map the target’s operations to applicable regional and industry regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, NYDFS, PCI-DSS) and evaluate their current compliance status.
9. **Audit & Certification Validation:** Collect and analyze recent third-party audit reports, certifications (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001), and regulatory exam findings to validate security claims and identify ongoing deficiencies.
10. **Data Privacy & Cross-Border Transfer Review:** Evaluate data localization requirements, consent mechanisms, and cross-border data transfer agreements (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses) to ensure legal data flow post-acquisition.
11. **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) Audit:** Review existing PIAs or Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) to understand how the target evaluates risk when launching new products or systems.

## Phase 4: Enterprise and Third-Party Risk Management
12. **Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) Analysis:** Audit the target's vendor risk management lifecycle, identifying critical sub-processors, supply chain dependencies, and high-risk third-party connections.
13. **Cyber Insurance Review:** Evaluate the target's current cyber liability insurance coverage, including limits, deductibles, exclusions, and adherence to policy prerequisites (like MFA enforcement).
14. **Quantitative Risk & Liability Modeling:** Translate discovered GRC gaps and compliance violations into estimated financial liabilities, regulatory fines, and required remediation capital.

## Phase 5: Human Capital and Security Culture
15. **Security Awareness Program Evaluation:** Assess the curriculum, completion rates, and effectiveness of the target’s employee security awareness training and anti-phishing campaigns.
16. **Background Check & Insider Threat Review:** Review the target's HR security policies, including pre-employment background checks, offboarding procedures, and insider threat monitoring capabilities.

## Phase 6: Post-Sign / Post-Close Integration & Harmonization
17. **Policy Harmonization:** Draft a unified set of Information Security Policies that bridge the gap between the acquired entity's existing culture and the parent organization's requirements.
18. **GRC Tooling Integration:** Plan the migration of the acquired entity's risk registers, vendor assessments, and compliance artifacts into the parent organization’s centralized GRC platform (e.g., Archer, ServiceNow GRC, AuditBoard).
19. **Establish Integration KPIs & Reporting:** Define clear Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) to track the progress of GRC integration efforts for executive stakeholders.
20. **Security Champion Onboarding:** Identify key personnel within the acquired entity to serve as "Security Champions," ensuring local advocacy and smooth adoption of the parent organization's governance standards.
