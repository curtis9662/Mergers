# Enterprise Security M&A Due Diligence: Top 20 Duties

When an enterprise acquires a new entity, assessing the target's cybersecurity posture is critical to identifying hidden liabilities, integration costs, and potential deal-breakers. Below are the top 20 duties for an Enterprise Security M&A Due Diligence service offering, structured across key security domains.

## Phase 1: Pre-Diligence & Scoping
1. **Define Due Diligence Scope & Strategy:** Tailor the security assessment framework based on the target entity’s industry, size, regulatory landscape, and the strategic rationale of the acquisition.
2. **Establish Secure Data Handling:** Create secure communication channels and utilize a secure Virtual Data Room (VDR) to protect sensitive security artifacts and intellectual property during the review process.

## Phase 2: Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)
3. **Policy & Documentation Review:** Evaluate the maturity of the target's foundational security documentation, including Information Security Policies, Acceptable Use, and Data Classification standards.
4. **Regulatory Compliance Assessment:** Assess the target’s adherence to applicable data privacy laws and industry regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, NYDFS).
5. **Audit & Certification Analysis:** Review historical external audits, SOC 2 Type II reports, ISO 27001 certifications, and regulatory exam findings to validate security claims.
6. **Third-Party Risk Management Evaluation:** Analyze the target's supply chain security, reviewing their vendor risk management program and critical third-party dependencies.

## Phase 3: Architecture, Cloud & Infrastructure
7. **Asset & Network Mapping:** Discover and inventory all critical physical, virtual, and cloud-based assets. Review network architecture diagrams to identify segmentation and border controls.
8. **Identity and Access Management (IAM) Review:** Evaluate processes for user provisioning, de-provisioning, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enforcement, and Privileged Access Management (PAM).
9. **Cloud Security Assessment:** Analyze the security configurations of the target’s cloud environments (AWS, Azure, GCP) and SaaS applications, looking for misconfigurations and data exposure risks.
10. **Data Protection & Cryptography:** Assess how the target protects its "crown jewels" (intellectual property, PII, PHI) both at rest and in transit using encryption and key management.

## Phase 4: Threat, Vulnerability & Application Security
11. **Vulnerability & Penetration Test Review:** Analyze recent internal/external vulnerability scans and penetration testing reports, focusing on the backlog of un-remediated high-risk findings.
12. **Threat Intelligence & OSINT Sweep:** Conduct Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and dark web research to identify compromised employee credentials, leaked data, or ongoing active threats against the target.
13. **Product & Application Security (AppSec):** If the target develops software, review their secure Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), DevSecOps practices, and Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) for vulnerable open-source libraries.

## Phase 5: Security Operations & Incident Response
14. **Historical Breach & Incident Analysis:** Review logs and records of past security incidents, data breaches, and the subsequent remediation steps taken by the target.
15. **Security Operations Center (SOC) Evaluation:** Assess the target's continuous monitoring capabilities, endpoint detection and response (EDR) deployments, and SIEM coverage.
16. **Incident Response & Resilience:** Evaluate the target's Incident Response Plan (IRP), Disaster Recovery (DR), and Business Continuity Plan (BCP), including the frequency of tabletop exercises and backups.

## Phase 6: Human Capital & Security Culture
17. **Security Awareness & Training:** Review the frequency, completion rates, and effectiveness of employee security awareness training and phishing simulation programs.
18. **Key Personnel Assessment:** Identify critical cybersecurity personnel (e.g., CISO, lead architects) and assess flight risks, as their departure post-acquisition could create significant vulnerabilities.

## Phase 7: Synthesis, Valuation & Integration Planning
19. **Quantify Remediation & Integration Costs:** Estimate the financial impact of remediating identified high-risk vulnerabilities and the capital required to integrate the target into the acquiring enterprise’s security architecture.
20. **Draft Final Due Diligence Report:** Compile a comprehensive executive summary and technical report detailing the risk profile, potential deal-breakers (red flags), and a prioritized roadmap for Post-Merger Integration (PMI).
